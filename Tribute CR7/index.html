Cristiano Ronaldo was born in Funchal, Madeira, Portugal, on February 5, 1985. He grew up in a poor family with three siblings and his father, who was a gardener and an equipment manager for a local football club. He developed a passion for football at an early age and started playing for Andorinha, a small amateur team, when he was seven years old. He later joined Nacional, a bigger club in Madeira, where he impressed with his skills and speed. He moved to Lisbon when he was 12 years old to join Sporting CP, one of the top clubs in Portugal, where he received his education and training at the club’s academy. He made his debut for the senior team when he was 17 years old in 2002, and scored two goals in his first game. He attracted the attention of several European clubs, such as Manchester United, Barcelona, and Real Madrid, with his dazzling performances and potential. He signed for Manchester United in 2003, after impressing Sir Alex Ferguson, the manager of the club, in a friendly match. He became the first Portuguese player to play for Manchester United and the most expensive teenager in English football history at the time.

Achivements of CR7:

  • He has won five Ballon d’Or awards, the most prestigious individual award for a football player
  • He has won four European Golden Shoes, the most by a European player.
  • He holds the records for most appearances (183), goals (140) and assists (42) in the Champions League, goals in the European Championship (14), international goals (128) and international appearances (205).
  • He is the only player to have scored 50 goals or more in the Premier League, La Liga, and Serie A, the three major European leagues1.
  • He is married to Georgina Rodríguez, a Spanish model and influencer, and has four children: Cristiano Jr., Mateo, Eva and Alana.
